# Spec Review — Inconsistencies, Bugs, Holes & Limitations

**Date:** 2026-06-12
**Reviewed artifact:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-26-game-mechanics.md` at commit `a4a1b10` (post hero-weapon demolition, sigils, inversion stat-math, destroy-mark resolution).
**Method:** full top-to-bottom read; every finding cites the section/line it lives at. **No spec edits were made** — this is a read-only audit. Each finding is tagged:

- **[CONTRADICTION]** — two spec passages that cannot both be true.
- **[BUG]** — a rule that, implemented as written, produces a wrong or clearly unintended outcome.
- **[HOLE]** — a case an implementer hits that the spec does not define.
- **[DRIFT]** — documentation inconsistency (stale text, example/table mismatch) with no rules ambiguity once spotted.
- **[LIMITATION]** — something the architecture cannot express and that is not yet recorded anywhere.

Severity: **H**igh (blocks or corrupts implementation), **M**edium (must be decided before its epic), **L**ow (cleanup).

## Part 1 — Findings

### A. Contradictions and bugs

1. **[CONTRADICTION, H] The ③ "target is alive" check forbids the save-from-lethal plays the rest of the spec is built around.** §4 ③ (line ~965): *"Target validity: target is alive, is not stealthed … and is a member of the declared selector's output."* But the `Dead…` selector family (§3 `ITargetSelector`, lines ~712/724) exists precisely to let save/finisher cards **target mortally-wounded minions** (`currentHealth ≤ 0`), and §4 ⑥′/Batching says the `All…/Alive…/Dead…` choice "decides whether a mortally-wounded match is offered — inclusive for a save card." A blanket aliveness gate at ③ rejects every such submission (`InvalidTarget` on a dying minion). The aliveness *policy* was supposed to be carried entirely by the selector family (validation = set membership); the ③ wording predates the trichotomy and was never updated. **Fix:** drop "is alive" from ③'s target-validity line; aliveness is whatever the declared selector says. (The *attacker* aliveness check is separate and correct.)

2. **[CONTRADICTION, H] The directed-events list leaks every drawn card to the opponent.** §2B "Directed events" (line ~336) enumerates the directed set as exactly `MulliganStartedEvent`, `ChoicePromptEvent`, `InterventionPromptEvent` — and says everything else "broadcasts to both players' wire streams." But `CardDrawnEvent { playerId, card }` and `CardAddedToHandEvent { playerId, card }` carry the **full card**. Broadcast as written, the opponent's wire contains every card you draw — a total hand reveal. The spec even contradicts itself: the ⑥′ visibility rule (line ~785) argues `causeEventIds[]` are safe because "a trigger matching the opponent's `CardDrawnEvent` does not expose the drawn card" — which is only true if that event is directed or thinned for the opponent. **Fix:** split draw/give events into directed-full + public-thin forms (the established `ChoicePromptEvent`/`ChoiceStartedEvent` pattern): opponent sees "drew a card," owner sees which. (`HandOverflowEvent`'s burned card and `CardDiscardedEvent` may deliberately stay public — HS reveals both.)

3. **[BUG, M] Poisonous is scoped to "a damaged *enemy* minion" — wrong under the lane model.** §3 `IKeyword` (line ~564): Poisonous "→ enqueue `DestroyMinionAction` on a damaged **enemy** minion." Under the lane-based predicates a **neutral minion is never anyone's enemy** — so a Poisonous minion retaliating against a commanded neutral attacker, or striking a neutral in the neutral lane, would not destroy it; nor would Poisonous ever kill a *friendly* minion damaged via redirect. HS Poisonous destroys **any minion it damages**, regardless of side. The hook has the damaged target in hand (`OnDealtDamage(sourceId, targetId, …)`) — relational filtering is gratuitous. **Fix:** "any minion it damages (heroes excepted)."

4. **[BUG/HOLE, M] Lifesteal heals "the source's owner" — undefined for a neutral source.** Same v1-hooks line: Lifesteal "→ enqueue `HealAction` for the source's owner." A neutral minion with Lifesteal (spawned with it, or aura-granted) that deals damage — retaliation, command, or its own trigger — has `ownerId == null`: there is no owner hero to heal. The hook silently breaks. **Fix:** define it — most consistent with the lane model is "no-op for an unowned source" (neutral has no hero, per §3's own "neutral has no hero" note), stated explicitly.

5. **[CONTRADICTION, M] `AttackDeclaredEvent` violates the "event log = state-delta stream" invariant.** §2B "Bus ⊋ log" (line ~334) is emphatic: the one non-delta signal is `ActionDeclaredEvent`, excluded from the persisted log/wire, so "the event log is exactly the state-delta stream stays true." But `AttackDeclaredEvent` (line ~380) is published at **③′ — before any state change** ("may still fizzle/retarget before ④") and sits in the ordinary §2B Combat table, i.e. in the wire log. It is intent, not delta — exactly the thing the invariant excludes. Either it must be excluded/bus-only too (breaking its stated "renderer telegraph" purpose), or the invariant's wording must carve out renderer-courtesy declaration events. As written, both claims can't hold.

6. **[BUG (philosophy)/HOLE, M] `isDamaged` is stored derived state — the exact pattern the spec eliminates elsewhere — and its maintenance is never specified.** `isDamaged` (§1, line ~87) is fully derivable as `currentHealth < maxHealth`. The spec deleted `canAttack` and `attacksAllowedThisTurn` *specifically because* stored verdicts drift from their sources, yet `isDamaged` persists with no stated update points: nothing says the `HealAction` handler clears it on full heal, what aura-driven `maxHealth` changes do to it, or what inversion's full-health landing sets it to. Enrage (⑥ recompute) reads it, so drift is gameplay-visible. **Fix:** either delete the field (pull the comparison at the two read points) or specify every write site.

7. **[DRIFT, L] The ⑤ publish-order text omits inscriptions.** §4 ⑤ (line ~996): "current player's list first (minions by current board index, then their artifacts by slot), then opponent's…" — written when artifacts landed, never updated for the inscription slot that §3 `IEventBus` (line ~494) and the Deterministic-Ordering table (line ~921) both define (minions → artifacts → **inscriptions**). Doc drift only, but ⑤ is the text an implementer of the bus loop will actually follow.

8. **[DRIFT, L] The trace worked example contradicts the spec's own vocabulary — and its own conventions.** §3 `ITraceSink` example (lines ~903–906) uses **`MinionDamagedEvent`** (no such event — §2B defines `DamageTakenEvent`), **`DeathrattleAction`** (no such action type in §2A), and field names `attacker`/`defender`/`src`/`tgt` where §2A defines `attackerId`/`targetId` — directly after the convention bullet that says "action/event payloads inline their §2 fields **verbatim**; the trace schema defers to §2."

9. **[DRIFT, L] `IAura.SourceMinionId` — but artifacts host auras now.** §1 `ArtifactOnBoard` (line ~94) and the §3 zone-hosting table (line ~753) say a passive artifact "may host an `IAura`," but the `IAura` contract (line ~818) exposes `SourceMinionId` and the registration prose speaks of `OnSummon`. Naming/lifecycle wording assumes minion hosts. Cosmetic until someone implements an artifact aura against the literal interface.

10. **[DRIFT, L] Assorted stale text.** (a) §3 `SpellDamageBonus` (line ~601): "cannot leak into combat or **hero-power** damage" — hero powers no longer exist (artifacts). (b) §3 `ITriggerCondition` (line ~630): `sourceId` is "the minion **or hero** the trigger belongs to" — heroes never host triggers; hosts are minions, cards (sigils), artifacts. (c) `StatModifier.sourceId` is documented as an entity ref, but the inversion memento writes the sentinel string `"inversion"` into it (§2A `InvertTargetAction`) — works, but breaks the field's stated type discipline.

### B. Holes — rules an implementer needs that don't exist

11. **[HOLE, H] Match setup is essentially unspecified.** The spec defines mulligan *machinery* (phase, `MulliganState`, events, ① shuffle/coin/deal under `IRandom`) but none of the *rules*: **starting hero health** (no constant anywhere — `PlayerState.health` has no stated initial value), **opening hand sizes** (first vs second player), **the mulligan procedure itself** (how many cards may be replaced, are replacements drawn before or after shuffling back, can mulliganed cards be redrawn), **going-second compensation** (extra card? a Coin-like token? nothing?), **deck construction constraints** (deck size, copies-per-card — even if deck *validation* is server-side, the engine asserts something at init), **turn-1 seeded mana values** (step 4 says setup seeds them; the values are never given), and the **`WaitingForPlayers` phase** (in the `GamePhase` enum but absent from the ② phase-guard table and from any transition description). Also: **opening-hand sigils** — hand-trigger registration is specified to happen "in the `DrawCard`/`GiveCard` handler," but the opening hand is dealt at setup, outside the action pipeline; when do those triggers register, and with what `birthEpoch`?

12. **[HOLE, H] Heroes have no maximum health — so hero healing is unbounded.** Minion healing is capped ("healed up to `maxHealth`," §1). `PlayerState` has `health` only; no `maxHealth`/starting value exists (see #11), so `HealAction` on a hero has no defined cap. HS caps at starting health (30); armor is the sanctioned over-cap mechanism — our armor model assumes the same division of labor but the cap itself is missing. Also worth one line: **how heroes are addressed as entities** — actions say "target = the player's hero," the trace uses `h1`, selectors return hero ids; the hero's entity-id convention (playerId? dedicated id?) is never defined.

13. **[HOLE, H] What `healthDelta` changes do to `currentHealth` is never defined.** The model derives `maxHealth = baseHealth + Σenchantments.healthDelta + auraHealthBonus`, and `currentHealth` "takes damage; healed up to maxHealth" — but nothing states the adjustment rule when the *derived max changes*: does gaining +0/+2 raise `currentHealth` by 2 (HS: yes)? When the buff/aura leaves, is `currentHealth` clamped to the new max, and does damage persist (HS: yes/yes)? Does Silence-stripping enchantments use the same clamp, with a minimum of 1 or not (HS: clamp, no minimum-1 for silence)? The only place the convention is even *implied* is the inversion handler stripping `auraHealthBonus` out of `currentHealth` (which presumes current *includes* the aura contribution). This rule is needed on day one of `BuffMinionAction`, `SilenceMinionAction`, and every aura pass; aura-loss deaths (`maxHealth ≤ 0` → pending death) already depend on it.

14. **[HOLE, M] `SilenceMinionAction` has no handler spec.** §1 field comments say what Silence clears (keywords, grantedKeywords, enchantments, grantedTribes), but the action row is bare and several semantics live nowhere: **does Silence unregister the minion's `ITrigger` subscriptions and Deathrattle** (HS: yes — and our triggers are bus subscriptions, so the handler must actively unsubscribe)? Does it clear `isFrozen` (HS: yes)? Does it interact with `summoningSick` (HS: no)? What does it do to the inversion damage-memento (answered in §2A `InvertTargetAction` — forgiven — but only discoverable there)? The currentHealth clamp on stripped health buffs is #13.

15. **[HOLE, M] `TransformMinionAction` is a bare row.** The epoch filter covers the trigger side ("a freshly … transformed minion does not trigger off the event that introduced it," §3 `IEventBus`), but nothing defines the state semantics: full reset to the new definition (HS) vs carryover of damage/enchantments; is the transformed body `summoningSick` (HS: cannot attack that turn); are old triggers unregistered and new ones registered with a fresh `birthEpoch`; does `summonOrder` reset; what happens to `isInverted` across a transform?

16. **[HOLE, H] "The responder" is never actually defined — and the hand side lacks the actor-attribution rule the inscription side got.** Every window passage says "the responder's matching hand cards" without saying **who the responder is**. Implicitly it is the non-acting player (the locked scope's "when an opponent declares an action, the other player gets a window"), but: (a) can the **acting player's own** hand sigils match their own action's events at ⑥′ (e.g. my AoE hits my own minions; I hold a "when a friendly minion takes damage" sigil) — is there a self-response window? The inscription side answers this explicitly (actor-attribution: never on own actions); the hand side never states it. (b) For **system/neutral-attributed actions** (turn-start neutral repopulation, a neutral minion's own trigger damaging someone), *both* players could hold matching sigils — who gets a window, just one, both in what order? `PendingIntervention` is structurally single-responder. The session-9 actor-attribution work fixed exactly this question for inscriptions and left hand windows untouched.

17. **[HOLE, M] Actor attribution is undefined for sourceless system actions — own-fatigue and own-draw spring your own inscriptions.** §3 Sigils defines attribution for player actions (submitter) and effect actions (source's controller), with neutral-sourced = unattributed = fires both sides. But the turn-start `DrawCardAction` and the fatigue `DealDamageAction { sourceId = null }` are **system actions with no source** — under the stated rule they are unattributed, so a player's own "when a card is drawn"/"when your hero takes damage" inscription fires **on their own draw/fatigue**, violating the rule's whole point ("never fires on events caused by its own owner's action"). **Fix:** extend attribution with a fallback — a sourceless system action is attributed to its `playerId` param when it has one; truly unowned otherwise.

18. **[HOLE, M] Are ③′/⑥′ active inside the death wave's own queue processing?** ⑦ Phase 2 says deathrattle actions are processed with "steps ④–⑥ run for each" — conspicuously omitting ③′ and ⑥′, while ⑨'s cascade-drain text says "①–⑥, **including ⑥′**." If windows are suppressed during Phase 2/3, deathrattle damage can never be responded to (no save against a deathrattle that mortally wounds a second minion — though next-wave deferral would make such a save *coherent* if allowed); if they're open, that should be stated, because responses during ⑦ interleave with wave bookkeeping. Either answer is implementable; the spec doesn't pick one.

19. **[HOLE, M] Neutral-zone capacity is never enforced.** `NeutralZoneConfig.maxCapacity` exists (§1), but no validation/fizzle rule consumes it: `SpawnNeutralMinionAction` into a full lane — reject, fizzle (the full-board-summon pattern), or overflow? `BoardFull` is defined as "player board has fewer than 7" and doesn't cover the lane. (Also note: the player-board cap "7" appears only as an inline number here — see #28.)

20. **[HOLE, L→M] Reborn summons "at 1 HP" through an action that has no HP parameter.** ⑦ Phase 3 enqueues `SummonMinionAction` "at 1 HP," but the §2A shape is `{ definitionKey, ownerId?, boardPosition, sourceId? }` — no health override field. Either the action gains an optional `currentHealthOverride` (then its general semantics need a sentence) or Reborn needs a dedicated summon variant.

21. **[HOLE, M] Bounce/give into a full hand is undefined.** `ReturnToHandAction` on a board minion when the owner's hand is full (HS: the minion is destroyed), and `GiveCardAction` into a full hand (HS: the card is burned/fizzles) — neither case is specified. `HandOverflowEvent` is defined only for the draw branch.

22. **[HOLE, L] `ModifyManaAction` clamping is unspecified.** Can `mana` exceed `maxMana` via a gain (HS: yes, temporary crystals — but our model has no such concept), or go below 0? One sentence each for floor/ceiling.

23. **[HOLE, M] Combo/`cardsPlayedThisTurn` accounting is ambiguous across the turn boundary.** (a) Lifecycle step 7 says "Reset `cardsPlayedThisTurn`" without saying **whose** (the artifact half of the same sentence specifies "the new active player's"). (b) Inscribing explicitly increments the counter (§2A `PlayCardAction`), but whether an **invocation** (`SubmitInterventionAction` — off-turn, "plays one card") increments the *invoker's* counter is never stated — it matters for Combo cards invoked or played after an off-turn invocation, and for any future "cards played this turn" reads.

24. **[HOLE, L] Timeout auto-resolution policy for `PendingChoice` is unstated.** The replay section correctly classifies timeout-injected actions as logged inputs, and intervention timeout = injected skip is pinned. But a timed-out `PendingChoice` (Discover, mid-effect targeting, **mulligan**) must inject a concrete `SubmitChoiceAction` — picking *what* (first option? random via `IRandom`? — if random, note it's the Game Server rolling, not the engine)? Replay is safe either way (the injected choice is logged); fairness/UX policy is not.

25. **[HOLE, L] The hand-sigil liveness drop-list is incomplete.** §1 `Card` / §3 hosting table: hand triggers are "dropped on **play/discard/return**" — omitting *shuffled from hand into deck* (`CardShuffledIntoDeckEvent` exists) and any future hand-exit route (transform-in-hand, crafting consumption). Read literally, a sigil shuffled into the deck keeps a live trigger. **Fix:** "dropped when the card leaves the hand by any route."

26. **[HOLE, L] The inscription empty-pool guard is checked at fire time, but the pool can empty before the response's ④.** §3 Sigils: an inscription "does not fire if a required selector pool is empty at fire time — stays inscribed." But fire-time ≠ resolution-time: the reveal consumes the card immediately, the response's enqueued action evaluates its selector at *its own* ④ later in the cascade, and the pool may have emptied in between → revealed-and-consumed-but-fizzled, the exact feel-bad the guard exists to prevent. Narrow (same-cascade pool changes), but the guard's timing guarantee should be stated honestly: it prevents *foreseeably*-empty fires only.

27. **[HOLE, M] Two window-opening mechanisms coexist, and `Start*` actions are themselves declarable.** §4 ③′ describes the engine **inline-suspending** the action and opening the window itself; §2A separately defines `StartInterventionAction` (with a `heldAction?` param) and `StartChoiceAction` as queueable actions whose ④ handlers emit the prompts. Which mechanism opens ⑥′ windows — engine checkpoint or enqueued action — is not pinned, and it matters: §2B `ActionDeclaredEvent` fires "for **every** dispatched action," so a queued `StartInterventionAction`/`StartChoiceAction` is itself **interceptable at ③′** — a sigil could cancel the *opening of a window* (or of a Discover), which is surely unintended recursion surface. **Fix:** pin one mechanism (engine-internal checkpoints; `Start*` reserved for effect-issued choices) and exempt window/choice-opening from declaration, or whitelist which action types declare.

28. **[DRIFT/HOLE, L] Constants hygiene.** Named: `GameConstants.MaxArtifacts = 3`, `MaxInscriptions = 3`, `MaxDeathWaves = 16`. Inline magic numbers: board cap **7** (③ prose only), mana cap **10** (lifecycle step 4 only). Missing entirely: hand cap (#11/#21 depend on it), starting health, opening-hand sizes, turn-timer seconds. One `GameConstants` block would close several of these at once.

29. **[DRIFT, L] `MinionStatsChangedEvent` is vestigial.** Documented as "(signals aura recalc needed)" — but recalc is unconditional per-action at ⑥; nothing subscribes to this event, and no handler is specified to emit it. Either give it a real producer/consumer or delete it.

30. **[⛔ MOOT — inversion parked to v2, 2026-06-14] `CardInvertedEvent` broadcasts a hand-zone state change.** *(Event deleted; finding no longer applies. Original text retained below.)* The session-8 visibility audit thinned Discover options and intervention candidates but didn't touch `CardInvertedEvent { cardId, playerId, isInverted }`: inverting a card **in hand** broadcasts the flip (opaque `cardId`, but the opponent still learns *a hand card inverted* — more than HS-style hidden hands reveal, and inconsistent with the inscribe-redaction standard). Candidate for the directed-full/public-thin split, or for accepting with a stated rationale.

### C. Limitations not yet recorded anywhere

31. **[LIMITATION, M] Inscriptions have zero counterplay surface.** No selector reaches the inscriptions zone, no action destroys/reveals/steals an inscription, and Silence doesn't apply (cards aren't minions). HS ships Flare/Eater-of-Secrets-style answers; we cannot express one. Deliberate-feeling, but unrecorded — it belongs in Unaddressed Features with a cost-to-enable (an `InscriptionSelector` surface + a `DestroyInscriptionAction`, both additive).

32. **[LIMITATION, L] The same sigil behaves differently against Stealth across its two modes — unstated.** Invoked, its player-chosen target is gated by `TargetStealthed` at ③; inscribed, the degraded **random-K** draw happens at ④ over the raw selector pool, which contains stealthed minions (selectors don't model Stealth). HS-consistent (random effects ignore Stealth) and arguably correct — but it's a behavioral mode-asymmetry of the unified card that the Sigils section's "same selector, same cardinality — the mode flips, the card doesn't" sentence actively obscures. Worth one explicit sentence either way.

33. **[LIMITATION, L] No mid-match hand-size/maximum-board interaction for inscriptions.** Inscribing frees a hand slot (stated); but nothing prevents *only* inscribe-locking: with 3 inscriptions down and 3 copies of other sigils in hand, the zone is the sole pressure valve (fine — just noting the cap interacts with #19/#28 constants once tuned).

### D. Found during the fix pass

34. **[recorded in the borrow-list note, not here]** The `PlayCardAction` → `ResolveCardAction` commit/resolve split (interception-cancel was a free no-op). User-found in session 10, applied in batch 1 (`375d40d`). Listed here only for numbering continuity; full record in the borrow-list "Spec-review fix pass" section.

35. **[CONTRADICTION/HOLE, M] Stealth-break (and attack-budget) timing vs interception — a *cancelled* attack currently keeps Stealth and its attack.** The `AttackAction` ④ handler (§2A) breaks Stealth (step 3) and consumes `attacksUsedThisTurn` (step 2) **inside ④**, *after* the ③′ interception window. So an attack **cancelled** at ③′ never reaches ④ → the attacker keeps Stealth and its attack budget. Intended behavior (user, 2026-06-14): **declaring** an attack reveals the minion, so an intercepted/cancelled attack should still break Stealth — the swing is prevented, not the reveal. (A *retarget* interception already proceeds to ④, so it breaks Stealth; the gap is the *cancel* case only.) Surfaced while correcting a stray "`AttackDeclaredEvent` is a renderer convenience" line — `AttackDeclaredEvent` is in fact mechanically load-bearing (the ③′ hook interception fires on), which is the whole point. **Resolution direction (to walk this session):** apply the **#34 pattern** to combat — `AttackAction` ④ *commits* the reveal (break Stealth + consume budget + `AttackPerformedEvent`) and enqueues a resolution (`ResolveAttackAction`-style) whose ③′ is the damage-interception window; "prevent the attack" then hooks `AttackAction` ③′ (nothing committed, Stealth survives), "counter the swing" hooks the resolution (already revealed) — structurally identical to the spell cast/counter split. **Open sub-questions:** budget-on-commit vs on-resolve; whether the retaliation `DealDamageAction` splits the same way; exact placement of `AttackDeclaredEvent`/`AttackPerformedEvent`; interaction with the existing "two independent `DealDamageAction`s" combat model.

   **▶ Reviewed + PARKED (2026-06-14, session 12) — current behavior KEPT, no spec edit.** Re-reading the `AttackAction` ④ handler showed the pipeline already has **two** interception points, with the reveal committing *between* them: `AttackAction` ③′ (cancel = "prevent the attack" → ④ never runs → Stealth + budget survive) → `AttackAction` ④ (commits snapshot + `attacksUsedThisTurn++` + Stealth-break + `AttackPerformedEvent`) → the enqueued **strike** `DealDamageAction` ③′ (cancel = "counter the swing" → Stealth *already* broke at ④). So **a swing-counter already breaks Stealth** — the original #35 framing misread "the interception" as a single point. This already mirrors the #34 spell prevent/counter duality (`AttackAction` ③′ ≈ prevent-the-cast, strike `DealDamageAction` ③′ ≈ counter). The *only* open case is whether a **declaration-cancel** ("prevent the attack") should *also* break Stealth; recommendation = **no** (parallel to prevent-the-cast — the attack never happened). **User's call: keep as-is for now, revisit if a card needs the other behavior.** No restructure; this finding is closed-as-accepted pending a future card requirement.

## Part 2 — Differences from Hearthstone

The spec is consciously HS-derived; this chapter inventories where it *departs*, grouped by direction of the change. (Faithful-by-design mechanics are listed last for completeness.)

### Systems Hearthstone does not have

- **The neutral lane.** A third board zone owned by neither player, with its own graveyard, spawn pool, optional turn-start repopulation, per-lane Taunt, and full participation in triggers/auras/selectors via the lane-based friendly/enemy model (`null == null` ⇒ a neutral's "friendlies" are its lane-mates; a neutral is *never anyone's enemy*). Players can attack neutrals, **command** them (card-granted one-shot attack, no zone change), or **control** them (permanent steal). HS has nothing comparable.
- **Inversion.** *(⛔ Parked to v2 on 2026-06-14 — no longer a v1 feature; see `notes/2026-06-14-inversion-v2.md`. Description retained for context.)* A reversible Normal/Inverted state on cards *and* minions: base stat pair swaps, enchantment deltas flip, the definition's inverted section swaps in different effects/triggers, and the pinned trade rule (`currentHealth` ↔ attack on aura-exclusive values) makes inverting a damaged minion convert damage into attack reduction at full flipped health. Enables the signature **invert-in-response-to-lethal** save. No HS analogue.
- **The intervention system / sigils.** HS has no priority windows at all — you can never act on the opponent's turn. Here, every reactive card (**sigil**) is dual-deployment: **invoked** from hand in a set-valued, MTG-style looping response window (re-declare/re-offer; pay-on-response from the carried pool), or **inscribed** face-down as an auto-firing one-shot. HS secrets are the nearest relative of inscriptions, but HS secrets are a separate card kind, hand-castable only as spells, and there is no choice-mode twin. The **choice/depth/economy** triangle (windows have choice but are depth-1-capped; inscriptions are choiceless but pierce response depth) is original design space.
- **Interception.** Declaration-phase responses (③′) that can `cancel`/`retarget` a pending action or mutate the board before re-validation — Counterspell exists in HS only as a secret; here it's a general, player-choice-capable phase on *every* action, combat damage included (Ice-Block-style prevention is an interception, not a death-replacement).
- **Artifacts.** A per-player row (cap 3) of non-combat entities — passive (triggers/auras), active (cost + target + effects), or both — with one durability counter, generic charges, free in-turn discard, and a shared escalating-cost **starter** ("pay mana, draw"). Replaces the hero-power system entirely (see below). HS has no persistent player-owned non-minion board entities of this kind (weapons were the closest, and we removed those too).
- **Aether / card crafting (v2, recorded only).** A planned third resource and component-purchase crafting flow — not in v1, but the spec reserves the seams (`notes/2026-06-11-card-crafting-v2.md`).

### Hearthstone systems deliberately removed

- **Heroes never attack.** No `heroAttack`, no weapons (the Weapon card type does not exist), no hero attack buffs, no hero Windfury math, no weapon-durability bookkeeping. Hero offense lives in the artifact row. Consequence, now structural: **hero defenders never retaliate** (retaliation is gated on defender attack > 0, and a hero has none).
- **No hero powers.** Replaced wholesale by artifacts; Inspire became `OnArtifactActivated`. Classes still exist (`heroClass` tied to the deck) but confer no unique button.
- **No hero freeze.** Freeze denies attacks; heroes have none to deny — `FreezeTargetAction` is minion-only. (HS freezes heroes to deny weapon swings.)
- **No spell schools, no overload, no temporary mana crystals** (the model notes where overload *would* slot — the turn-end refresh — but none of these exist in v1).

### Shared mechanics with modified behavior

- **Combat is two sequential damage actions, not one atomic exchange.** HS applies both combat damages simultaneously, then processes deaths/triggers. Here the strike and the retaliation are independent `DealDamageAction`s, each with its own ③′ and ⑥′ window; a reaction can interleave **between the hits** (e.g. bounce the defender to dodge its retaliation), and a "when damaged" reaction sees per-hit board state. Death simultaneity is preserved via the shared settle wave — both combatants still die together. This is the spec's one *acknowledged* divergence from HS's apply-both-then-react.
- **Death timing is HS-faithful (cascade-settle) but exploitable by design.** Like HS, deaths process in batched waves after the cascade; *unlike* HS, the mortally-wounded window is a **designer surface**: dying minions are targetable/countable/keyword-active, the `All…/Alive…/Dead…` selector trichotomy partitions on it, and save-from-lethal (heal or invert above 0) is a supported play pattern. HS has no way for a player to act inside that window.
- **Two doom kinds with different counterplay.** Damage-doom is savable until the settle; **destroy-marks are final and silent** (no event, no window) and preventable only by intercepting the destroy action's declaration. HS destroys are equally final but HS also lacks the save window for damage — our split (health dooms savable at the consequence, fiat dooms preventable at the cause) is new vocabulary.
- **Mana refreshes at turn END, not turn start.** Outcome-identical on your own turn, but the refreshed pool sits through the opponent's turn so **off-turn responses spend real mana** — a concept HS cannot express at all (it has no off-turn actions). Affordability of responses is naturally capped by your full ramped next-turn pool.
- **Per-lane Taunt.** Taunt constrains attacks *into its own lane only*: a neutral Taunt locks attacks aimed at the neutral lane but never protects the opponent, and vice versa. HS Taunt is global across the single enemy side (and our opponent-lane half behaves exactly like HS, hero included).
- **Secrets-vs-inscriptions constraints.** One-per-name (HS-faithful), but cap **3** (HS: 5), pay-on-inscribe with public mana delta (HS-faithful), **actor-attribution** instead of HS's "opponent's turn only" rule — indistinguishable on normal turns, but our inscriptions correctly fire on an opponent's *invocation during your own turn*, a case HS's rule can't even encounter. Choice-requiring cards degrade to engine-random targeting when inscribed — HS secrets are all choice-free by construction, so the question never arises there.
- **Fatigue is HS-exact in math** (1, 2, 3, … escalating, never resets) but **routes through the ordinary damage channel** — so armor absorbs it (true in HS too) *and* it is interceptable/reactable like any damage (impossible in HS).
- **Freeze cost is HS's net behavior, achieved differently** ("costs exactly one attack" via the end-of-ending-turn thaw sweep keyed on `frozenOnTurn` + exhaustion, rather than HS's start-of-turn rules).
- **Stealth, Divine Shield, Windfury, Charge/Rush, Reborn, Poisonous, Lifesteal, Spell Damage, Enrage, Combo, Discover, Mulligan, 10-mana cap, 7-minion boards** — all present with HS-standard player-visible behavior (implementation models differ: pulled keywords, derived attack budgets, declarative effective views). Where this review found the *implementations* diverging from HS behavior unintentionally, that's findings #3 (Poisonous side-scope) and #13 (buff/health interaction — HS's rules are the obvious fill-in).
- **Armor** is HS-faithful (partial absorb before health, no cap, fatigue-absorbing) with one wire-visible difference: absorption is a field on the damage event (`armorAbsorbed`), not a separate armor-loss event.
- **Randomness and replay are stricter than HS needs:** counter-based per-action RNG derivation, a canonical input log (including timeout-injected actions), and full determinism guarantees — invisible to players, but they shape several rules above (e.g. inscribed-mode random targeting is deterministic given the seed).

### Net effect, in one paragraph

Relative to Hearthstone, this game *removes* the hero as a combatant and redistributes that power budget into board-adjacent systems (artifacts, the neutral lane), then *adds* a true off-turn response economy (sigils/interventions riding the turn-end mana refresh) that HS structurally cannot have. The deepest rule-level innovations — the designer-exploitable dying window, the two-doom counterplay split, inversion as both a stat mechanic and a save mechanic, and dual-deployment reactive cards — all hang off one architectural choice HS also made but never exposed to players: deaths settle late. The spec keeps HS's feel everywhere it doesn't deliberately depart, and the departures are consistently in the direction of *more counterplay during the opponent's turn*.
