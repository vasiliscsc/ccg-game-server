# Spec Review ‚Äî Inconsistencies, Bugs, Holes & Limitations

**Date:** 2026-06-12
**Reviewed artifact:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-26-game-mechanics.md` at commit `a4a1b10` (post hero-weapon demolition, sigils, inversion stat-math, destroy-mark resolution).
**Method:** full top-to-bottom read; every finding cites the section/line it lives at. **No spec edits were made** ‚Äî this is a read-only audit. Each finding is tagged:

- **[CONTRADICTION]** ‚Äî two spec passages that cannot both be true.
- **[BUG]** ‚Äî a rule that, implemented as written, produces a wrong or clearly unintended outcome.
- **[HOLE]** ‚Äî a case an implementer hits that the spec does not define.
- **[DRIFT]** ‚Äî documentation inconsistency (stale text, example/table mismatch) with no rules ambiguity once spotted.
- **[LIMITATION]** ‚Äî something the architecture cannot express and that is not yet recorded anywhere.

Severity: **H**igh (blocks or corrupts implementation), **M**edium (must be decided before its epic), **L**ow (cleanup).

## Part 1 ‚Äî Findings

### A. Contradictions and bugs

1. **[CONTRADICTION, H] The ‚ë¢ "target is alive" check forbids the save-from-lethal plays the rest of the spec is built around.** ¬ß4 ‚ë¢ (line ~965): *"Target validity: target is alive, is not stealthed ‚Ä¶ and is a member of the declared selector's output."* But the `Dead‚Ä¶` selector family (¬ß3 `ITargetSelector`, lines ~712/724) exists precisely to let save/finisher cards **target mortally-wounded minions** (`currentHealth ‚â§ 0`), and ¬ß4 ‚ë•‚Ä≤/Batching says the `All‚Ä¶/Alive‚Ä¶/Dead‚Ä¶` choice "decides whether a mortally-wounded match is offered ‚Äî inclusive for a save card." A blanket aliveness gate at ‚ë¢ rejects every such submission (`InvalidTarget` on a dying minion). The aliveness *policy* was supposed to be carried entirely by the selector family (validation = set membership); the ‚ë¢ wording predates the trichotomy and was never updated. **Fix:** drop "is alive" from ‚ë¢'s target-validity line; aliveness is whatever the declared selector says. (The *attacker* aliveness check is separate and correct.)

2. **[CONTRADICTION, H] The directed-events list leaks every drawn card to the opponent.** ¬ß2B "Directed events" (line ~336) enumerates the directed set as exactly `MulliganStartedEvent`, `ChoicePromptEvent`, `InterventionPromptEvent` ‚Äî and says everything else "broadcasts to both players' wire streams." But `CardDrawnEvent { playerId, card }` and `CardAddedToHandEvent { playerId, card }` carry the **full card**. Broadcast as written, the opponent's wire contains every card you draw ‚Äî a total hand reveal. The spec even contradicts itself: the ‚ë•‚Ä≤ visibility rule (line ~785) argues `causeEventIds[]` are safe because "a trigger matching the opponent's `CardDrawnEvent` does not expose the drawn card" ‚Äî which is only true if that event is directed or thinned for the opponent. **Fix:** split draw/give events into directed-full + public-thin forms (the established `ChoicePromptEvent`/`ChoiceStartedEvent` pattern): opponent sees "drew a card," owner sees which. (`HandOverflowEvent`'s burned card and `CardDiscardedEvent` may deliberately stay public ‚Äî HS reveals both.)

3. **[BUG, M] Poisonous is scoped to "a damaged *enemy* minion" ‚Äî wrong under the lane model.** ¬ß3 `IKeyword` (line ~564): Poisonous "‚Üí enqueue `DestroyMinionAction` on a damaged **enemy** minion." Under the lane-based predicates a **neutral minion is never anyone's enemy** ‚Äî so a Poisonous minion retaliating against a commanded neutral attacker, or striking a neutral in the neutral lane, would not destroy it; nor would Poisonous ever kill a *friendly* minion damaged via redirect. HS Poisonous destroys **any minion it damages**, regardless of side. The hook has the damaged target in hand (`OnDealtDamage(sourceId, targetId, ‚Ä¶)`) ‚Äî relational filtering is gratuitous. **Fix:** "any minion it damages (heroes excepted)."

4. **[BUG/HOLE, M] Lifesteal heals "the source's owner" ‚Äî undefined for a neutral source.** Same v1-hooks line: Lifesteal "‚Üí enqueue `HealAction` for the source's owner." A neutral minion with Lifesteal (spawned with it, or aura-granted) that deals damage ‚Äî retaliation, command, or its own trigger ‚Äî has `ownerId == null`: there is no owner hero to heal. The hook silently breaks. **Fix:** define it ‚Äî most consistent with the lane model is "no-op for an unowned source" (neutral has no hero, per ¬ß3's own "neutral has no hero" note), stated explicitly.

5. **[CONTRADICTION, M] `AttackDeclaredEvent` violates the "event log = state-delta stream" invariant.** ¬ß2B "Bus ‚äã log" (line ~334) is emphatic: the one non-delta signal is `ActionDeclaredEvent`, excluded from the persisted log/wire, so "the event log is exactly the state-delta stream stays true." But `AttackDeclaredEvent` (line ~380) is published at **‚ë¢‚Ä≤ ‚Äî before any state change** ("may still fizzle/retarget before ‚ë£") and sits in the ordinary ¬ß2B Combat table, i.e. in the wire log. It is intent, not delta ‚Äî exactly the thing the invariant excludes. Either it must be excluded/bus-only too (breaking its stated "renderer telegraph" purpose), or the invariant's wording must carve out renderer-courtesy declaration events. As written, both claims can't hold.

6. **[BUG (philosophy)/HOLE, M] `isDamaged` is stored derived state ‚Äî the exact pattern the spec eliminates elsewhere ‚Äî and its maintenance is never specified.** `isDamaged` (¬ß1, line ~87) is fully derivable as `currentHealth < maxHealth`. The spec deleted `canAttack` and `attacksAllowedThisTurn` *specifically because* stored verdicts drift from their sources, yet `isDamaged` persists with no stated update points: nothing says the `HealAction` handler clears it on full heal, what aura-driven `maxHealth` changes do to it, or what inversion's full-health landing sets it to. Enrage (‚ë• recompute) reads it, so drift is gameplay-visible. **Fix:** either delete the field (pull the comparison at the two read points) or specify every write site.

7. **[DRIFT, L] The ‚ë§ publish-order text omits inscriptions.** ¬ß4 ‚ë§ (line ~996): "current player's list first (minions by current board index, then their artifacts by slot), then opponent's‚Ä¶" ‚Äî written when artifacts landed, never updated for the inscription slot that ¬ß3 `IEventBus` (line ~494) and the Deterministic-Ordering table (line ~921) both define (minions ‚Üí artifacts ‚Üí **inscriptions**). Doc drift only, but ‚ë§ is the text an implementer of the bus loop will actually follow.

8. **[DRIFT, L] The trace worked example contradicts the spec's own vocabulary ‚Äî and its own conventions.** ¬ß3 `ITraceSink` example (lines ~903‚Äì906) uses **`MinionDamagedEvent`** (no such event ‚Äî ¬ß2B defines `DamageTakenEvent`), **`DeathrattleAction`** (no such action type in ¬ß2A), and field names `attacker`/`defender`/`src`/`tgt` where ¬ß2A defines `attackerId`/`targetId` ‚Äî directly after the convention bullet that says "action/event payloads inline their ¬ß2 fields **verbatim**; the trace schema defers to ¬ß2."

9. **[DRIFT, L] `IAura.SourceMinionId` ‚Äî but artifacts host auras now.** ¬ß1 `ArtifactOnBoard` (line ~94) and the ¬ß3 zone-hosting table (line ~753) say a passive artifact "may host an `IAura`," but the `IAura` contract (line ~818) exposes `SourceMinionId` and the registration prose speaks of `OnSummon`. Naming/lifecycle wording assumes minion hosts. Cosmetic until someone implements an artifact aura against the literal interface.

10. **[DRIFT, L] Assorted stale text.** (a) ¬ß3 `SpellDamageBonus` (line ~601): "cannot leak into combat or **hero-power** damage" ‚Äî hero powers no longer exist (artifacts). (b) ¬ß3 `ITriggerCondition` (line ~630): `sourceId` is "the minion **or hero** the trigger belongs to" ‚Äî heroes never host triggers; hosts are minions, cards (sigils), artifacts. (c) `StatModifier.sourceId` is documented as an entity ref, but the inversion memento writes the sentinel string `"inversion"` into it (¬ß2A `InvertTargetAction`) ‚Äî works, but breaks the field's stated type discipline.

### B. Holes ‚Äî rules an implementer needs that don't exist

11. **[HOLE, H] Match setup is essentially unspecified.** The spec defines mulligan *machinery* (phase, `MulliganState`, events, ‚ëÝ shuffle/coin/deal under `IRandom`) but none of the *rules*: **starting hero health** (no constant anywhere ‚Äî `PlayerState.health` has no stated initial value), **opening hand sizes** (first vs second player), **the mulligan procedure itself** (how many cards may be replaced, are replacements drawn before or after shuffling back, can mulliganed cards be redrawn), **going-second compensation** (extra card? a Coin-like token? nothing?), **deck construction constraints** (deck size, copies-per-card ‚Äî even if deck *validation* is server-side, the engine asserts something at init), **turn-1 seeded mana values** (step 4 says setup seeds them; the values are never given), and the **`WaitingForPlayers` phase** (in the `GamePhase` enum but absent from the ‚ë° phase-guard table and from any transition description). Also: **opening-hand sigils** ‚Äî hand-trigger registration is specified to happen "in the `DrawCard`/`GiveCard` handler," but the opening hand is dealt at setup, outside the action pipeline; when do those triggers register, and with what `birthEpoch`?

12. **[HOLE, H] Heroes have no maximum health ‚Äî so hero healing is unbounded.** Minion healing is capped ("healed up to `maxHealth`," ¬ß1). `PlayerState` has `health` only; no `maxHealth`/starting value exists (see #11), so `HealAction` on a hero has no defined cap. HS caps at starting health (30); armor is the sanctioned over-cap mechanism ‚Äî our armor model assumes the same division of labor but the cap itself is missing. Also worth one line: **how heroes are addressed as entities** ‚Äî actions say "target = the player's hero," the trace uses `h1`, selectors return hero ids; the hero's entity-id convention (playerId? dedicated id?) is never defined.

13. **[HOLE, H] What `healthDelta` changes do to `currentHealth` is never defined.** The model derives `maxHealth = baseHealth + Œ£enchantments.healthDelta + auraHealthBonus`, and `currentHealth` "takes damage; healed up to maxHealth" ‚Äî but nothing states the adjustment rule when the *derived max changes*: does gaining +0/+2 raise `currentHealth` by 2 (HS: yes)? When the buff/aura leaves, is `currentHealth` clamped to the new max, and does damage persist (HS: yes/yes)? Does Silence-stripping enchantments use the same clamp, with a minimum of 1 or not (HS: clamp, no minimum-1 for silence)? The only place the convention is even *implied* is the inversion handler stripping `auraHealthBonus` out of `currentHealth` (which presumes current *includes* the aura contribution). This rule is needed on day one of `BuffMinionAction`, `SilenceMinionAction`, and every aura pass; aura-loss deaths (`maxHealth ‚â§ 0` ‚Üí pending death) already depend on it.

14. **[HOLE, M] `SilenceMinionAction` has no handler spec.** ¬ß1 field comments say what Silence clears (keywords, grantedKeywords, enchantments, grantedTribes), but the action row is bare and several semantics live nowhere: **does Silence unregister the minion's `ITrigger` subscriptions and Deathrattle** (HS: yes ‚Äî and our triggers are bus subscriptions, so the handler must actively unsubscribe)? Does it clear `isFrozen` (HS: yes)? Does it interact with `summoningSick` (HS: no)? What does it do to the inversion damage-memento (answered in ¬ß2A `InvertTargetAction` ‚Äî forgiven ‚Äî but only discoverable there)? The currentHealth clamp on stripped health buffs is #13.

15. **[HOLE, M] `TransformMinionAction` is a bare row.** The epoch filter covers the trigger side ("a freshly ‚Ä¶ transformed minion does not trigger off the event that introduced it," ¬ß3 `IEventBus`), but nothing defines the state semantics: full reset to the new definition (HS) vs carryover of damage/enchantments; is the transformed body `summoningSick` (HS: cannot attack that turn); are old triggers unregistered and new ones registered with a fresh `birthEpoch`; does `summonOrder` reset; what happens to `isInverted` across a transform?

16. **[HOLE, H] "The responder" is never actually defined ‚Äî and the hand side lacks the actor-attribution rule the inscription side got.** Every window passage says "the responder's matching hand cards" without saying **who the responder is**. Implicitly it is the non-acting player (the locked scope's "when an opponent declares an action, the other player gets a window"), but: (a) can the **acting player's own** hand sigils match their own action's events at ‚ë•‚Ä≤ (e.g. my AoE hits my own minions; I hold a "when a friendly minion takes damage" sigil) ‚Äî is there a self-response window? The inscription side answers this explicitly (actor-attribution: never on own actions); the hand side never states it. (b) For **system/neutral-attributed actions** (turn-start neutral repopulation, a neutral minion's own trigger damaging someone), *both* players could hold matching sigils ‚Äî who gets a window, just one, both in what order? `PendingIntervention` is structurally single-responder. The session-9 actor-attribution work fixed exactly this question for inscriptions and left hand windows untouched.

17. **[HOLE, M] Actor attribution is undefined for sourceless system actions ‚Äî own-fatigue and own-draw spring your own inscriptions.** ¬ß3 Sigils defines attribution for player actions (submitter) and effect actions (source's controller), with neutral-sourced = unattributed = fires both sides. But the turn-start `DrawCardAction` and the fatigue `DealDamageAction { sourceId = null }` are **system actions with no source** ‚Äî under the stated rule they are unattributed, so a player's own "when a card is drawn"/"when your hero takes damage" inscription fires **on their own draw/fatigue**, violating the rule's whole point ("never fires on events caused by its own owner's action"). **Fix:** extend attribution with a fallback ‚Äî a sourceless system action is attributed to its `playerId` param when it has one; truly unowned otherwise.

18. **[HOLE, M] Are ‚ë¢‚Ä≤/‚ë•‚Ä≤ active inside the death wave's own queue processing?** ‚ë¶ Phase 2 says deathrattle actions are processed with "steps ‚ë£‚Äì‚ë• run for each" ‚Äî conspicuously omitting ‚ë¢‚Ä≤ and ‚ë•‚Ä≤, while ‚ë®'s cascade-drain text says "‚ëÝ‚Äì‚ë•, **including ‚ë•‚Ä≤**." If windows are suppressed during Phase 2/3, deathrattle damage can never be responded to (no save against a deathrattle that mortally wounds a second minion ‚Äî though next-wave deferral would make such a save *coherent* if allowed); if they're open, that should be stated, because responses during ‚ë¶ interleave with wave bookkeeping. Either answer is implementable; the spec doesn't pick one.

19. **[HOLE, M] Neutral-zone capacity is never enforced.** `NeutralZoneConfig.maxCapacity` exists (¬ß1), but no validation/fizzle rule consumes it: `SpawnNeutralMinionAction` into a full lane ‚Äî reject, fizzle (the full-board-summon pattern), or overflow? `BoardFull` is defined as "player board has fewer than 7" and doesn't cover the lane. (Also note: the player-board cap "7" appears only as an inline number here ‚Äî see #28.)

20. **[HOLE, L‚ÜíM] Reborn summons "at 1 HP" through an action that has no HP parameter.** ‚ë¶ Phase 3 enqueues `SummonMinionAction` "at 1 HP," but the ¬ß2A shape is `{ definitionKey, ownerId?, boardPosition, sourceId? }` ‚Äî no health override field. Either the action gains an optional `currentHealthOverride` (then its general semantics need a sentence) or Reborn needs a dedicated summon variant.

21. **[HOLE, M] Bounce/give into a full hand is undefined.** `ReturnToHandAction` on a board minion when the owner's hand is full (HS: the minion is destroyed), and `GiveCardAction` into a full hand (HS: the card is burned/fizzles) ‚Äî neither case is specified. `HandOverflowEvent` is defined only for the draw branch.

22. **[HOLE, L] `ModifyManaAction` clamping is unspecified.** Can `mana` exceed `maxMana` via a gain (HS: yes, temporary crystals ‚Äî but our model has no such concept), or go below 0? One sentence each for floor/ceiling.

23. **[HOLE, M] Combo/`cardsPlayedThisTurn` accounting is ambiguous across the turn boundary.** (a) Lifecycle step 7 says "Reset `cardsPlayedThisTurn`" without saying **whose** (the artifact half of the same sentence specifies "the new active player's"). (b) Inscribing explicitly increments the counter (¬ß2A `PlayCardAction`), but whether an **invocation** (`SubmitInterventionAction` ‚Äî off-turn, "plays one card") increments the *invoker's* counter is never stated ‚Äî it matters for Combo cards invoked or played after an off-turn invocation, and for any future "cards played this turn" reads.

24. **[HOLE, L] Timeout auto-resolution policy for `PendingChoice` is unstated.** The replay section correctly classifies timeout-injected actions as logged inputs, and intervention timeout = injected skip is pinned. But a timed-out `PendingChoice` (Discover, mid-effect targeting, **mulligan**) must inject a concrete `SubmitChoiceAction` ‚Äî picking *what* (first option? random via `IRandom`? ‚Äî if random, note it's the Game Server rolling, not the engine)? Replay is safe either way (the injected choice is logged); fairness/UX policy is not.

25. **[HOLE, L] The hand-sigil liveness drop-list is incomplete.** ¬ß1 `Card` / ¬ß3 hosting table: hand triggers are "dropped on **play/discard/return**" ‚Äî omitting *shuffled from hand into deck* (`CardShuffledIntoDeckEvent` exists) and any future hand-exit route (transform-in-hand, crafting consumption). Read literally, a sigil shuffled into the deck keeps a live trigger. **Fix:** "dropped when the card leaves the hand by any route."

26. **[HOLE, L] The inscription empty-pool guard is checked at fire time, but the pool can empty before the response's ‚ë£.** ¬ß3 Sigils: an inscription "does not fire if a required selector pool is empty at fire time ‚Äî stays inscribed." But fire-time ‚âÝ resolution-time: the reveal consumes the card immediately, the response's enqueued action evaluates its selector at *its own* ‚ë£ later in the cascade, and the pool may have emptied in between ‚Üí revealed-and-consumed-but-fizzled, the exact feel-bad the guard exists to prevent. Narrow (same-cascade pool changes), but the guard's timing guarantee should be stated honestly: it prevents *foreseeably*-empty fires only.

27. **[HOLE, M] Two window-opening mechanisms coexist, and `Start*` actions are themselves declarable.** ¬ß4 ‚ë¢‚Ä≤ describes the engine **inline-suspending** the action and opening the window itself; ¬ß2A separately defines `StartInterventionAction` (with a `heldAction?` param) and `StartChoiceAction` as queueable actions whose ‚ë£ handlers emit the prompts. Which mechanism opens ‚ë•‚Ä≤ windows ‚Äî engine checkpoint or enqueued action ‚Äî is not pinned, and it matters: ¬ß2B `ActionDeclaredEvent` fires "for **every** dispatched action," so a queued `StartInterventionAction`/`StartChoiceAction` is itself **interceptable at ‚ë¢‚Ä≤** ‚Äî a sigil could cancel the *opening of a window* (or of a Discover), which is surely unintended recursion surface. **Fix:** pin one mechanism (engine-internal checkpoints; `Start*` reserved for effect-issued choices) and exempt window/choice-opening from declaration, or whitelist which action types declare.

28. **[DRIFT/HOLE, L] Constants hygiene.** Named: `GameConstants.MaxArtifacts = 3`, `MaxInscriptions = 3`, `MaxDeathWaves = 16`. Inline magic numbers: board cap **7** (‚ë¢ prose only), mana cap **10** (lifecycle step 4 only). Missing entirely: hand cap (#11/#21 depend on it), starting health, opening-hand sizes, turn-timer seconds. One `GameConstants` block would close several of these at once.

29. **[DRIFT, L] `MinionStatsChangedEvent` is vestigial.** Documented as "(signals aura recalc needed)" ‚Äî but recalc is unconditional per-action at ‚ë•; nothing subscribes to this event, and no handler is specified to emit it. Either give it a real producer/consumer or delete it.

30. **[‚õî MOOT ‚Äî inversion parked to v2, 2026-06-14] `CardInvertedEvent` broadcasts a hand-zone state change.** *(Event deleted; finding no longer applies. Original text retained below.)* The session-8 visibility audit thinned Discover options and intervention candidates but didn't touch `CardInvertedEvent { cardId, playerId, isInverted }`: inverting a card **in hand** broadcasts the flip (opaque `cardId`, but the opponent still learns *a hand card inverted* ‚Äî more than HS-style hidden hands reveal, and inconsistent with the inscribe-redaction standard). Candidate for the directed-full/public-thin split, or for accepting with a stated rationale.

### C. Limitations not yet recorded anywhere

31. **[LIMITATION, M] Inscriptions have zero counterplay surface.** No selector reaches the inscriptions zone, no action destroys/reveals/steals an inscription, and Silence doesn't apply (cards aren't minions). HS ships Flare/Eater-of-Secrets-style answers; we cannot express one. Deliberate-feeling, but unrecorded ‚Äî it belongs in Unaddressed Features with a cost-to-enable (an `InscriptionSelector` surface + a `DestroyInscriptionAction`, both additive).

32. **[LIMITATION, L] The same sigil behaves differently against Stealth across its two modes ‚Äî unstated.** Invoked, its player-chosen target is gated by `TargetStealthed` at ‚ë¢; inscribed, the degraded **random-K** draw happens at ‚ë£ over the raw selector pool, which contains stealthed minions (selectors don't model Stealth). HS-consistent (random effects ignore Stealth) and arguably correct ‚Äî but it's a behavioral mode-asymmetry of the unified card that the Sigils section's "same selector, same cardinality ‚Äî the mode flips, the card doesn't" sentence actively obscures. Worth one explicit sentence either way.

33. **[LIMITATION, L] No mid-match hand-size/maximum-board interaction for inscriptions.** Inscribing frees a hand slot (stated); but nothing prevents *only* inscribe-locking: with 3 inscriptions down and 3 copies of other sigils in hand, the zone is the sole pressure valve (fine ‚Äî just noting the cap interacts with #19/#28 constants once tuned).

35. **[CONTRADICTION/HOLE, M] Stealth-break (and attack-budget) timing vs interception ‚Äî a *cancelled* attack currently keeps Stealth and its attack.** The `AttackAction` ‚ë£ handler (¬ß2A) breaks Stealth (step 3) and consumes `attacksUsedThisTurn` (step 2) **inside ‚ë£**, *after* the ‚ë¢‚Ä≤ interception window. ## Part 2 ‚Äî Differences from Hearthstone

The spec is consciously HS-derived; this chapter inventories where it *departs*, grouped by direction of the change. (Faithful-by-design mechanics are listed last for completeness.)

### Systems Hearthstone does not have

- **The neutral lane.** A third board zone owned by neither player, with its own graveyard, spawn pool, optional turn-start repopulation, per-lane Taunt, and full participation in triggers/auras/selectors via the lane-based friendly/enemy model (`null == null` ‚áí a neutral's "friendlies" are its lane-mates; a neutral is *never anyone's enemy*). Players can attack neutrals, **command** them (card-granted one-shot attack, no zone change), or **control** them (permanent steal). HS has nothing comparable.
- **Inversion.** *(‚õî Parked to v2 on 2026-06-14 ‚Äî no longer a v1 feature; see `notes/2026-06-14-inversion-v2.md`. Description retained for context.)* A reversible Normal/Inverted state on cards *and* minions: base stat pair swaps, enchantment deltas flip, the definition's inverted section swaps in different effects/triggers, and the pinned trade rule (`currentHealth` ‚Üî attack on aura-exclusive values) makes inverting a damaged minion convert damage into attack reduction at full flipped health. Enables the signature **invert-in-response-to-lethal** save. No HS analogue.
- **The intervention system / sigils.** HS has no priority windows at all ‚Äî you can never act on the opponent's turn. Here, every reactive card (**sigil**) is dual-deployment: **invoked** from hand in a set-valued, MTG-style looping response window (re-declare/re-offer; pay-on-response from the carried pool), or **inscribed** face-down as an auto-firing one-shot. HS secrets are the nearest relative of inscriptions, but HS secrets are a separate card kind, hand-castable only as spells, and there is no choice-mode twin. The **choice/depth/economy** triangle (windows have choice but are depth-1-capped; inscriptions are choiceless but pierce response depth) is original design space.
- **Interception.** Declaration-phase responses (‚ë¢‚Ä≤) that can `cancel`/`retarget` a pending action or mutate the board before re-validation ‚Äî Counterspell exists in HS only as a secret; here it's a general, player-choice-capable phase on *every* action, combat damage included (Ice-Block-style prevention is an interception, not a death-replacement).
- **Artifacts.** A per-player row (cap 3) of non-combat entities ‚Äî passive (triggers/auras), active (cost + target + effects), or both ‚Äî with one durability counter, generic charges, free in-turn discard, and a shared escalating-cost **starter** ("pay mana, draw"). Replaces the hero-power system entirely (see below). HS has no persistent player-owned non-minion board entities of this kind (weapons were the closest, and we removed those too).
- **Aether / card crafting (v2, recorded only).** A planned third resource and component-purchase crafting flow ‚Äî not in v1, but the spec reserves the seams (`notes/2026-06-11-card-crafting-v2.md`).

### Hearthstone systems deliberately removed

- **Heroes never attack.** No `heroAttack`, no weapons (the Weapon card type does not exist), no hero attack buffs, no hero Windfury math, no weapon-durability bookkeeping. Hero offense lives in the artifact row. Consequence, now structural: **hero defenders never retaliate** (retaliation is gated on defender attack > 0, and a hero has none).
- **No hero powers.** Replaced wholesale by artifacts; Inspire became `OnArtifactActivated`. Classes still exist (`heroClass` tied to the deck) but confer no unique button.
- **No hero freeze.** Freeze denies attacks; heroes have none to deny ‚Äî `FreezeTargetAction` is minion-only. (HS freezes heroes to deny weapon swings.)
- **No spell schools, no overload, no temporary mana crystals** (the model notes where overload *would* slot ‚Äî the turn-end refresh ‚Äî but none of these exist in v1).

### Shared mechanics with modified behavior

- **Combat is two sequential damage actions, not one atomic exchange.** HS applies both combat damages simultaneously, then processes deaths/triggers. Here the strike and the retaliation are independent `DealDamageAction`s, each with its own ‚ë¢‚Ä≤ and ‚ë•‚Ä≤ window; a reaction can interleave **between the hits** (e.g. bounce the defender to dodge its retaliation), and a "when damaged" reaction sees per-hit board state. Death simultaneity is preserved via the shared settle wave ‚Äî both combatants still die together. This is the spec's one *acknowledged* divergence from HS's apply-both-then-react.
- **Death timing is HS-faithful (cascade-settle) but exploitable by design.** Like HS, deaths process in batched waves after the cascade; *unlike* HS, the mortally-wounded window is a **designer surface**: dying minions are targetable/countable/keyword-active, the `All‚Ä¶/Alive‚Ä¶/Dead‚Ä¶` selector trichotomy partitions on it, and save-from-lethal (heal or invert above 0) is a supported play pattern. HS has no way for a player to act inside that window.
- **Two doom kinds with different counterplay.** Damage-doom is savable until the settle; **destroy-marks are final and silent** (no event, no window) and preventable only by intercepting the destroy action's declaration. HS destroys are equally final but HS also lacks the save window for damage ‚Äî our split (health dooms savable at the consequence, fiat dooms preventable at the cause) is new vocabulary.
- **Mana refreshes at turn END, not turn start.** Outcome-identical on your own turn, but the refreshed pool sits through the opponent's turn so **off-turn responses spend real mana** ‚Äî a concept HS cannot express at all (it has no off-turn actions). Affordability of responses is naturally capped by your full ramped next-turn pool.
- **Per-lane Taunt.** Taunt constrains attacks *into its own lane only*: a neutral Taunt locks attacks aimed at the neutral lane but never protects the opponent, and vice versa. HS Taunt is global across the single enemy side (and our opponent-lane half behaves exactly like HS, hero included).
- **Secrets-vs-inscriptions constraints.** One-per-name (HS-faithful), but cap **3** (HS: 5), pay-on-inscribe with public mana delta (HS-faithful), **actor-attribution** instead of HS's "opponent's turn only" rule ‚Äî indistinguishable on normal turns, but our inscriptions correctly fire on an opponent's *invocation during your own turn*, a case HS's rule can't even encounter. Choice-requiring cards degrade to engine-random targeting when inscribed ‚Äî HS secrets are all choice-free by construction, so the question never arises there.
- **Fatigue is HS-exact in math** (1, 2, 3, ‚Ä¶ escalating, never resets) but **routes through the ordinary damage channel** ‚Äî so armor absorbs it (true in HS too) *and* it is interceptable/reactable like any damage (impossible in HS).
- **Freeze cost is HS's net behavior, achieved differently** ("costs exactly one attack" via the end-of-ending-turn thaw sweep keyed on `frozenOnTurn` + exhaustion, rather than HS's start-of-turn rules).
- **Stealth, Divine Shield, Windfury, Charge/Rush, Reborn, Poisonous, Lifesteal, Spell Damage, Enrage, Combo, Discover, Mulligan, 10-mana cap, 7-minion boards** ‚Äî all present with HS-standard player-visible behavior (implementation models differ: pulled keywords, derived attack budgets, declarative effective views). Where this review found the *implementations* diverging from HS behavior unintentionally, that's findings #3 (Poisonous side-scope) and #13 (buff/health interaction ‚Äî HS's rules are the obvious fill-in).
- **Armor** is HS-faithful (partial absorb before health, no cap, fatigue-absorbing) with one wire-visible difference: absorption is a field on the damage event (`armorAbsorbed`), not a separate armor-loss event.
- **Randomness and replay are stricter than HS needs:** counter-based per-action RNG derivation, a canonical input log (including timeout-injected actions), and full determinism guarantees ‚Äî invisible to players, but they shape several rules above (e.g. inscribed-mode random targeting is deterministic given the seed).

### Net effect, in one paragraph

Relative to Hearthstone, this game *removes* the hero as a combatant and redistributes that power budget into board-adjacent systems (artifacts, the neutral lane), then *adds* a true off-turn response economy (sigils/interventions riding the turn-end mana refresh) that HS structurally cannot have. The deepest rule-level innovations ‚Äî the designer-exploitable dying window, the two-doom counterplay split, inversion as both a stat mechanic and a save mechanic, and dual-deployment reactive cards ‚Äî all hang off one architectural choice HS also made but never exposed to players: deaths settle late. The spec keeps HS's feel everywhere it doesn't deliberately depart, and the departures are consistently in the direction of *more counterplay during the opponent's turn*.
